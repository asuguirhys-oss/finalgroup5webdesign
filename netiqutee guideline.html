🍪 Netiquette Guidelines

Be kind. Be clear. Be cookie-classy online.

What is Netiquette?

Netiquette is the set of good manners for communicating online. It’s about remembering the human, being respectful, and using your words to build—not break—communities.

Golden Rule: Write to others the way you’d like others to write to you.

🍪 The 10 Core Rules

Each rule below expands—tap to read details and tips.

Rule 1: Remember the Human Respect real people behind the screens

Your words are read by real people. Before you hit Send, ask: “Would I be okay receiving this?”

Try: “Hi! I appreciate your point. Here’s another angle…”
Rule 2: Follow real-life standards Don’t be meaner online

If you wouldn’t say it face-to-face, don’t post it. Avoid SHOUTING in ALL CAPS, excessive slang, and insults.

Write: “Please / Thank you / I disagree because…”
Rule 3: Know where you are in cyberspace Match the space’s tone

Different spaces have different norms. A DM to a friend ≠ a class forum or work email.

Formal in class/work; casual with friends (still respectful!).
Rule 4: Respect people’s time & bandwidth Be concise and useful

Keep messages clear and focused. Avoid giant attachments and long off-topic text.

Use bullets, short paragraphs, and descriptive subject lines.
Rule 5: Make yourself look good online Clarity, grammar, and kindness
  • Check spelling and grammar.
  • Know your topic and state it clearly.
  • Be pleasant and polite.
If you wouldn’t show it in public, don’t share it online—today or ever.
Rule 6: Share expert knowledge Help the community learn

Post helpful resources and what you’ve learned. If you ask a question and get answers, share a summary back.

“Here are 3 links that solved my issue…”
Rule 7: Keep flame wars under control Cool heads, warm cookies

Strong opinions are okay; personal attacks aren’t. Don’t feed the flames—recenter the discussion.

“Let’s focus on the problem. What does the data say?”
Rule 8: Respect privacy Not everything is sharable

Treat private info with care. When unsure, don’t forward, screenshot, or repost.

Ask permission before sharing messages or photos.
Rule 9: Don’t abuse power Expertise ≠ license to bully

Skill and status come with responsibility. Use them to lift others, not to intimidate.

Offer guidance, not gatekeeping.
Rule 10: Be forgiving of mistakes Correct in private, praise in public

Everyone is learning. If it’s minor, let it slide. If you must correct, do it kindly, preferably in private.

“Small typo—thought you’d want to know. Thanks for the post!”
