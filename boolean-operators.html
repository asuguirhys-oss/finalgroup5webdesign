What Are Boolean Operators?

Boolean operators are simple words (AND, OR, NOT) that help refine search results. They were introduced by George Boole and are essential in logic, computer science, and online searching.

AND Operator

AND Returns results that contain both terms. narrows your search. Only results that include both terms will show.

Example: Cookies AND Brownies

OR Operator

OR Returns results that contain either term. broadens your search. Results can include either term.

Example: Cookies OR Brownies

NOT Operator

NOT Excludes the second term from the results. excludes a term. Results will contain the first term but not the second.

Example: Cookies NOT Brownies
